Motivations
I decided I wanted to build a personal computer after working with HP B2600 workstations for Syncom. This was before the price explosions of RAM and graphics cards, and I felt it would be cool to do my school assignments on a machine I built myself, rather than on Colab from my laptop.
Components
Processor: Intel i5 13600KF
Graphics Card: Sparkle 'ROC' Intel A770
Memory and Storage: Crucial 5600 mHz 32GB Kit and 1TB SSD
Memory and Storage: Crucial 5600 mHz 32GB Kit and a 1TB 'P3' SSD
Motherboard: Gigabyte Aorus Pro B760I (mini-ITX)
Case and Cooling: Corsair 2000D Airflow and an NZXT Kraken 360
Experience
This case was on closing sale for $60 and is one of the few ITX cases that can fit a 360mm AIO. Theres a side rail that comes with it that is explicitly designed to fit a full size cooler, which I thought was really cool. The A770 is a weak card, but has good vRAM capacity and decent overclocking capacity. It's the cheapest way to get 16GB of vRAM, but has overall performance that is now a couple generations old. Overall this is a functional build for the time being, I will likely build another one soon when prices return to feasible ranges.
